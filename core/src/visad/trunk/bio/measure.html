The Measure Tab

The Measure tab provides functions for measuring aspects of the data by placing lines and markers on and across data slices.

Basic measurement controls

To add a measurement line, click the "New line" button. To add a measurement marker, click the "New marker" button.

To select a measurement, click on it. Selecting multiple measurements is accomplished by holding SHIFT while clicking, or by holding SHIFT and dragging a rectangle over the measurements to select. Selected measurements have yellow endpoints, and selected lines appear dashed instead of solid. To unselect all measurements, click the left button on an empty area of the display. Note that in the current version of VisBio, measurements can be selected only in the 2-D display.

Once a measurement is selected, many of its properties can be altered. It can be removed by clicking the "Remove" button, its color can be changed using the "Color" drop-down box, its measurement group can be changed using the "Group" drop-down box (see the "Measurement groups" section), and it can be set as standard (see the "Standard measurements" section).

Lastly, to remove all measurements and start from scratch, click the "Clear all" button. A dialog will appear asking you to confirm your choice.

Measurement groups

A measurement group is a simple way to classify and group measurements. Each measurement can belong to a group with associated title and description. For example, if you use markers to label cell nuclei, you could make a group called "nuclei" by clicking the "New" button next to the "Group" drop-down box, provide a group description by typing it into the "Group description" text area, and assign each measurement to that group accordingly.

Standard measurements

A measurement can be set to exist on multiple slices or timesteps. Such a measurement is said to be "standard." A "2-D standard" measurement exists on every slice of every timestep, whereas a "3-D standard" measurement exists only once at every timestep (and not necessarily on the same slices at each step).

For example, suppose you wish to measure the diameter of a cell membrane at every slice of every timestep of your data. Place a line measuring the diameter at the first slice of the first timestep, then select it. Choose "2-D standard" to convert the measurement to a 2-D standard line. Then, proceed through each slice of each timestep, adjusting the line to match the cell membrane appropriately.

Standard measurements are particularly useful because they are represented in a special format when exported (see the "Exporting measurements" section).

Exporting measurements

To export the list of measurements to a tab-delineated text format appropriate for import into a spreadsheet program such as Excel, click the "Export measurements" button. In this file, standard measurement values will appear in a table format, and single measurements will appear as a list at the bottom.

Merging measurement endpoints

The endpoints from multiple measurement lines can be merged, so that dragging the merged endpoint adjusts all linked lines accordingly. To merge endpoints, click the "Merge" button. After being clicked, the button will appear highlighted. Then, hold SHIFT and drag a rectangle across all endpoints to be merged. All endpoints within this rectangle will be combined into a single endpoint.
